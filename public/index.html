I have extensive experience using Jquery as utility library complementing it when necessary with other libraries like UnderscoreJS

In the last two years I have been working in production environments with AngularJS, KnockoutJS and SpineJS. I've been also done some mockups with Backbone

Generally I create interactions optimized for smoothness and low power consumption, relying as much as I can in CSS transitions and animations, so I can keep Interaction, presentation and content layers separated.

Videogame programming is one of my hobbies and lately I have been exploring several HTML5/JS game engines and frameworks such as Phaser, ImpactJS or EaselJS; that has been quite useful to learn new ways of structuring the code and to note that they could be easily used as webapps development engines.

HTML & CSS

I'm pretty good & fast at writing clean, efficient, and semantically correct HTML & CSS.

I always apply graceful degradation philosophy to my works, due to my extensive experience I almost unconsciously apply styling patterns that result in posterior little or no crossbrowser debugging.

I have quite a good understanding of the pros and cons of SMACSS/Object Oriented CSS and have plenty of experience using (and customising) them in production environments.

I have experience with both SCSS and LESS and I'm pretty aware of the best practices to make them turn into a clean and efficient compiled CSS.
